# Land Cover Change Metric Dictionary

This document defines the columns generated by the granular Land Cover Change analysis (`analysis/LC_change_granular.qmd`).

## Overview

The analysis runs two independent models: 1. **Forest Loss Model**: Binary classification (Forest vs. Non-Forest). 2. **Expansion Model**: Multi-class classification (Urban, Cropland, Other).

Metrics are calculated comparing 1992 to 2020.

## Column Naming Convention

Format: `{Model}_{Metric}_{Category}_{YearRange}`

-   **Model**: `ForestLoss` or `Expansion`
-   **Metric**: `Gain`, `Loss`, `Persistence`, `Quantity`, `Exchange`, `Shift`, `dir_ch`
-   **Category**: The land cover class being analyzed (e.g., `Forest`, `Urban`)
-   **YearRange**: `2020_1992` (Indicates the comparison interval between these two years)

## Key Columns for Analysis

These are the most relevant metrics for attributing Ecosystem Service hotspots.

| Column Name | Interpretation | Relevance |
|:-------------------------------|:-------------------|:-------------------|
| **`ForestLoss_Loss_Forest_...`** | **Gross Deforestation**. The specific area of Forest that converted to Non-Forest. | **High**. Primary driver for biodiversity and carbon loss. |
| **`ForestLoss_Gain_Forest_...`** | **Gross Reforestation**. The area of Non-Forest that converted to Forest. | **High**. Potential recovery signal. |
| **`ForestLoss_dir_ch_Forest_...`** | **Net Forest Change**. (Gain - Loss). Negative values indicate net deforestation. | **High**. Summary metric. |
| **`Expansion_Gain_Urban_...`** | **Urbanization**. The area converted to Urban from any other class. | **High**. Driver of coastal risk, N-export, etc. |
| **`Expansion_Gain_Cropland_...`** | **Agricultural Expansion**. The area converted to Cropland from any other class. | **High**. Driver of sediment, N-export, pollination loss. |

## Detailed Metric Definitions

| Metric | Definition |
|:-----------------------------------|:-----------------------------------|
| **Gain** | The area of the category at the end time (2020) that was *not* that category at the start time (1992). |
| **Loss** | The area of the category at the start time (1992) that is *not* that category at the end time (2020). |
| **Persistence** | The area that remained the same category from 1992 to 2020. |
| **dir_ch** | Directional Change: `Gain - Loss`. Represents the net change in quantity. |
| **Quantity** | Net difference in area size (absolute value of Gain - Loss). |
| **Exchange** | The amount of area that swapped locations (e.g., forest lost in one corner of the grid cell but gained in another). Represents dynamic change with no net quantity change. |
| **Shift** | Change attributable to shift in location (often related to Exchange). |

## Redundant / Secondary Columns

-   `*_Overall_...`: Aggregates change across all categories in the model. Less useful for specific attribution.
-   `ForestLoss_Gain_NonForest`: Mathematically equivalent to `ForestLoss_Loss_Forest` (in a binary closed system). Use `Loss_Forest` for clarity.
-   `Expansion_Loss_Other`: Represents the source of expansion (loss of natural/semi-natural land). Useful context, but `Gain_Urban` / `Gain_Cropland` are the direct drivers.

## Interpretation Strategy

How to map these metrics to the "Drivers of Change" analysis:

### 1. The Attribution Gap

-   **Question:** How much of the decline in Ecosystem Services (ES) is due to direct land conversion?
-   **Method:** Calculate the % overlap between **ES Hotspots** (e.g., Nitrogen Export increase) and **Conversion Hotspots** (top 5% of `ForestLoss_Loss_Forest` or `Expansion_Gain_...`).
-   **Interpretation:**
    -   **High Overlap:** The ES decline is driven by land use change (e.g., deforestation). Policy response: Protection, Land Use Planning.
    -   **Low Overlap:** The ES decline is likely driven by **degradation** (e.g., thinning, climate stress) or **intensification** (e.g., more fertilizer on existing cropland) rather than area loss. Policy response: Management practices, Restoration.

### 2. Driver Identification

-   **Question:** Is the conversion driven by Urbanization or Agriculture?
-   **Method:** Compare the correlation of ES change with `Expansion_Gain_Urban` vs. `Expansion_Gain_Cropland`.
-   **Hypothesis:**
    -   **Coastal Risk** should correlate strongly with `Expansion_Gain_Urban` (coastal hardening).
    -   **Sediment/Nitrogen** should correlate strongly with `Expansion_Gain_Cropland` (agricultural expansion).
